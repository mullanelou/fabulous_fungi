WELCOME

The Castletroy area of Limerick, like many regions in Ireland, hosts a diverse range of fungi species throughout the year. In its woodlands, you can encounter various mushroom varieties, including the classic field mushroom (Agaricus campestris) and the aromatic chanterelles (Cantharellus cibarius). In the fall, the damp forests might reveal a treasure trove of boletes, such as the Bay Bolete (Imleria badia) and the Penny Bun (Boletus edulis). On decaying wood and trees, bracket fungi like the Turkey Tail (Trametes versicolor) add a burst of color to the landscape. The occasional sighting of jelly fungi like Wood Ear (Auricularia auricula-judae) or lichens adorning rocks and trees further enrich the mycological tapestry of Castletroy. While exploring this area's natural beauty, one must always exercise caution when identifying fungi, as some can be toxic, making it essential to consult with local experts or field guides when delving into the world of mushrooms in Castletroy.
